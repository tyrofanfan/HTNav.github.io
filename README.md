## Abstract

Inspired by the general Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN) task, aerial VLN has attracted increasing attention due to its practical value in logistics delivery, urban inspection, and other real-world applications. However, existing methods still struggle in complex urban environments, suffering from limited generalization to unseen scenes, suboptimal long-horizon planning, and insufficient modeling of spatial continuity.

To address these challenges, we propose **HTNav**, a collaborative navigation framework that integrates Imitation Learning (IL) and Reinforcement Learning (RL) in a hybrid IL-RL paradigm. HTNav employs a staged training strategy to stabilize the base navigation policy while improving exploration. A tiered decision-making mechanism coordinates macro-level route planning with fine-grained action control, and a map representation learning module further enhances spatial continuity understanding in open domains. On the CityNav benchmark, HTNav achieves state-of-the-art results across all scene levels and task difficulties, demonstrating improved precision and robustness for urban aerial VLN.
